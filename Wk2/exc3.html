CSYM023

This is a dissertation module, i provide the student with the opportunity to:

  • acquire basic research skills,
  • carry out sustained independent work
  • select an area for further investigation and present a project proposal based around a candidate research question
  • refine the research question as improved understanding of theory and practice is achieved
  • select and justify the paradigm and/or methodology chosen
  • develop and extend their critical and evaluative skills·
  • produce a dissertation thesis of about 15,000 words and defend the final outcome.

CSYM015

To teach students the fundamental theory and practical applications of search methods and agents.The underpinning concepts will be introduced followed by examples of how intelligent systems are used in applications on the Internet.

CSYM017

Databases represent a vital resource in the vast majority of computer systems, particularly Internet-based systems. This module is designed for students to study different database systems – from the traditional relational databases to recent NoSQL databases, etc. This module starts with some basic database concepts and modelling skills and quickly moves on to more advanced concepts and systems to cover the required breadth and depth of the module. There are plenty of built in practical tasks for students to reinforce their learning.

CSYM025

This module concentrates on creating object-based solutions to software problems within a contemporary integrated development environment. It focuses on the concepts of object technology and the syntax and semantics of the underlying model while promoting good practice in software construction and testing.
